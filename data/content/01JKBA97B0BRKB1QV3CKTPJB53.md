From across glass rectangles, the homba peer at the tiny ship rocked by a violent wake. The hrududu leaves its suffocating, oily embleer as it sails past Dander and Freckle, who glare back, tharn with terror.

After journeying northward for a long time, they find a quiet bay where the hrududu dare not come and drop a heavy metal hook over the side. They watch the sun set over liquid silver, knowing the elil to be far away and that before them lie the narn days, endless and warm.

## May 2026

![a hand drawn ink illustration of 2 bears dancing together, with others watching in the background, an hommage to William Holbrook Beard's Dancing Bears, painted in 1865](../media/content/videos/may_2026.jpg)

*   **Wiki**, documented the installation of Pino's [water tank](../site/water_tank.html), added [thermostat](../site/engine_care.html#thermostat) to [engine care](engine_care.html), and updated [woodstove installation](../site/woodstove_installation.html#may2026).
*   **[Grimgrains](grimgrains.html)**, added a [solar-roasted kinako](https://grimgrains.com/site/solar_roasted_kinako.html) recipe.
*   **[Uxn](uxn.html)**, added instructions for validating builds during the [bootstrap process](https://wiki.xxiivv.com/site/uxntal_bootstrap).
*   **[No Bears None](no_bears_none.html)**, we released a book! Nearly everything else was pushed aside so that we could complete it.

In the last few days before our departure, we finished rebuilding our galley cabinet and installed the new [water tank](../site/water_tank.html). As per usual, we took our bikes apart, filled our bins with fresh produce, stowed away everything, and finally, on May 17th, propelled by a strong tide and a desire to be in a [quiet place](https://rabbits.srht.site/days/2026/05/23.html), Pino flew away from the crowds of Victoria.

These days, every morning at 0400, we are awakened by purple martins perched on our lifelines singing their dawn song, after coffee we write, draw, row, wander ashore, and we try to roast new things in the sun, like [soy beans](https://grimgrains.com/site/solar_roasted_kinako.html).

On the 25th, we finally released the first edition of [No Bears None](no_bears_none.html) for e-readers, a very detailed logbook about our sail from Victoria to Sitka. It's a heavily edited version of the original [Victoria to Sitka logbook](victoria_to_sitka_logbook.html), with a ton of extra stuff like a 100 drawings, 17 topical chapters and 13 recipes. A big thank you to everyone who has already got their copy! We plan to release a paperback version, either late this year or beginning of 2027.

Rek designed the [shirt](https://kokorobot.ca/media/content/iwp9_shirt.jpg) for this year's [International Workshop on Plan 9](https://iwp9.org/) that happened in Victoria.

Devine implemented a [vector renderer](https://wiki.xxiivv.com/site/hershey), which allows us to do beautiful typesetting directly in [Uxn](uxn.html). We used that to release two zines this month, one for helping folks who want to break into [Interactions Nets](https://wiki.xxiivv.com/site/pocket_nets), and another one called [Fractions Are Everything](https://wiki.xxiivv.com/site/pocket_fractions) that invite readers to look at fractions differently.

**Book Club:** We are reading _Sailing Back in Time_ by Maria Coffey, Emily Wilson's translation of _The Odyssey_ and _Heart Of Darkness_ by Joseph Conrad.

## April 2026

![a tribute to MC Eschers piece Bond of Union. Two rabbits, both are in different states of unravel, one more advanced than the other, white spheres are afloat around the 2 rabbits.](../media/content/videos/april_2026.jpg)

*   **Wiki**, documented the replacement of Pino's [headstay](../site/standing_rigging_replacement.html#apr2026).
*   **[Rabbit Waves](rabbit_waves.html)**, added a new page about [Ship Lights](https://rabbitwaves.ca/site/ship_lights.html).

Summer's already here, pushing us to finish a few last minute projects aboard [Pino](pino.html). Between all the sewing, sanding, varnishing, greasing, climbing up the mast, climbing up the mast some more, dropping tools in the water, cabinet-making(?), we're just about ready to meet it shipshape.

After taking the [furler](../site/standing_rigging_replacement.html#apr2026) apart into a thousand little pieces and putting it all back together(without losing too many pieces), we raised our new shiny spotless [jib](headsail.html). It looks fantastic, except that now everything else looks awfully dirty... we'll get to the rest eventually.

A few days ago, we looked at our home, with the new standing rigging, headsail, new lights, filled with food, and with less than 2 weeks to go before we cast off, and thought, okay, we have time for one last big project: ripping one of the old galley cabinets apart to rebuild it, and to install a new water tank. After ten years on Pino there are very few places aboard unknown to us, now there is sawdust on everything and we finally know what lies between the galley and cockpit locker. Before we rebuild the cabinet, we plan to pass a 100L(23 Gallon) water tank through this gap to install it in the locker.

While playtesting the new [Donsol](donsol.html), we found a few things that could be improved with the [Uxn](uxn.html) Playdate emulator, so we fixed it and pushed a new version of [Oquonie](oquonie.html) to the Catalog. The game should feel smoother, and ah, yes, there was this wall you could walk through... well, now that's fixed too.

Devine has been haunted by a cursed idea we hinted at back in February. It has come together this month into a notation called [Rejoice](https://wiki.xxiivv.com/site/rejoice.html). We've got a brief cursory description, an implementation and a few examples to demonstrate what it is. It has been fun to puzzle over what it can be used to express.

Relatedly, Giuseppe Peano whose work has directly inspired this project, wrote some of his work in a language he called as Latino Sine Flexione. The idea was to strip Classical Latin down to its simplest possible grammar while keeping its vocabulary recognizable. As a kind of tribute to Peano's work, the Rejoice documentation was translated into that language. Devine tried to emulate Peano's writing style as closely as possible, and .. yeah. Mileage may vary.

By the way, thanks to everyone who reached out with ideas for Rejoice, and helped put it all together, except for that one guy who forwarded us what ChatGPT had to say about it. Don't be that guy.

**Book Club:** We read Orwell's _1984_, and are still reading _Island_ by Aldous Huxley.

This is likely very obvious to most people, but recently it dawned on us that clothes are, in a certain way, open-source. We can go to a thrift store, buy a shirt, rip out the seams, study it, lay all of the pieces down over new fabric, trace the patterns, cut them out, and sew it all back together to produce a copy of that same shirt. We've been wanting to learn how to make our own clothes but didn't know where to begin, until this month. Our first project was to reproduce our hats, after the store we bought them at stopped stocking them. We bought black cotton canvas at a local fabric store, took our old hats apart and re-built them(See [Rek's hat](https://rabbits.srht.site/days/2026/01/11.html), and [Dev's hat](https://rabbits.srht.site/days/2026/01/17.html)). Later, Devine did the same for our old [messenger backpack](https://wiki.xxiivv.com/site/commuter_backpack.html).

Goblin Week, an event in which people draw goblins for 7 days, occurs on the last full week of January. Devine's [entries](https://wiki.xxiivv.com/site/ghostly_chanting.html) were inspired by a passage in Samuel Butler's [Erewhon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erewhon), and Rek drew some spunky cartoony [goblins](https://kokorobot.ca/site/goblins.html#2026).
