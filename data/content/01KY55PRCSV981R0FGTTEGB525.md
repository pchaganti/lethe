Most of us walk through museums reading the little labels and nodding. Name, date, oil on canvas. This is the rest of it: the eight questions art historians actually ask, asked out loud on the paintings everyone knows.

a sheets.works story

## The labels don’t help

A museum label gives you a name, a date, and the words oil on canvas. It doesn’t mention that the couple’s shoes are off, or that two extra people are hiding in the mirror.

That information exists. It sits in catalogues and monographs written for people who already have the degree. So most of us stand in front of a famous painting for about a minute, nod, and move on.

## Watch it work once

This is the back wall of the Arnolfini Portrait, 1434. The whole room repeats in the convex mirror, and two people stand in its doorway who appear nowhere else in the painting. They’re standing roughly where you are.

Above the mirror the painter wrote _Johannes de eyck fuit hic_. Jan van Eyck was here. Painters normally wrote _made this_. He signed it like a witness.

Nobody spots the doorway people unaided. Everyone sees them once they’re pointed at. That’s the whole product.

## The eight questions

They work on a Bruegel and they work on a postcard. The order matters: look first, interpret later.

1**what is this**the name, the painter, the year. One sentence, out loud.

2**first look**list what’s there before deciding what any of it means.

3**who is here**count everyone. People, animals, reflections, backs of heads.

4**what is happening**one sentence you could defend while pointing at the paint.

5**the objects**pick two things that look deliberate and ask what job they do.

6**how it’s built**where does your eye land first, and where does it go next?

7**when and where**who could afford this, and who was meant to see it?

8**the bit that sticks**the one thing you’ll still remember next week.

## Nine paintings, read closely

Start anywhere. Each read takes about three minutes, the camera does the pointing, and the painting is yours to explore at the end. More are on the way.
